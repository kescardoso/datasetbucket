  • How we analyze your data

    Location: If your data has a Location tag, we look to see if all locations are equally represented. String and number formats accepted.

    Gender and/or Sex: If your data has a Gender or Sex tag, we look to see if all genders are equally represented. For example, if you only have two genders in your data, we will recommend that you consider adding data to include other genders. Only string inputs are accepted. If values are numbers, they will be treated as strings.

    Ethnicity: If your data has a Ethnicity tag, we look to see if all ethnicities are equally represented. The distribution of your data should be proportional to the distribution in the population the data is from. Only string inputs are accepted.

    Religion: If your data has a Religion tag, we look to see if all religions are equally represented. The distribution of your data should be proportional to the distribution in the population the data is from. Only string inputs are accepted.

    Income: If your data has an Income tag, we look to see if all Income are proportionally represented. The distribution of your data should be proportional to the distribution in the population the data is from. Only number inputs are accepted.

    Age: If your data has an Age tag, we look to see if all ages are proportionally represented. The distribution of your data should be proportional to the distribution in the population the data is from. Only number inputs are accepted.

    Education: If your data has an Education tag, we look to see if all education levels are proportionally represented. The distribution of your data should be proportional to the distribution in the population the data is from. Only string inputs are accepted.

  • Bias

    Defined as: prejudice in favor of or against one thing, person, or group compared with another, usually in a way considered to be unfair

  • Sample Bias

    Sample bias occurs when a dataset does not reflect the realities of the environment in which a model will run.

  • Measurement Bias

    This type of bias occurs when the data collected for training differs from that collected in the real world, or when faulty measurements result in data distortion.

  • Racial Bias

    Though not data bias in the traditional sense, this still warrants mentioning due to its prevalence in AI technology of late. Racial bias occurs when data skews in favor of particular demographics. This can also be extended to gender bias.
